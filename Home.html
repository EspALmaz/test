J. K. Rowling, known under the pseudonyms J. K. Rowling, J. K. Rowling and Robert Galbraith— is a British writer, screenwriter and film producer, best known as the author of the Harry Potter series of novels. The Harry Potter books have won several awards and have sold over 500 million copies. They became the best-selling series of books in history and the basis for a series of films, which became the third highest-grossing series of films in history. J.K. Rowling herself approved the scripts of the films, and also joined the producers of the last two parts.

The magical world of Harry Potter is a fictional world in which the action of a series of novels about the young wizard Harry Potter takes place. The author of the world, as well as a series of books, is the English writer J.K. Rowling.The actions of the magical world unfold on Earth, mainly in England in the 1990s. To be more precise, the first book begins with the murder of Harry Potter's parents in 1981, and in 1991 he enters the first year of Hogwarts. Accordingly, the main plot of the series covers the period from 1991 to 1998. The epilogue with the adult main characters at the train station takes place in 2017. In this setting, wizards secretly live among ordinary people who have the ability to use magic and the ability to use magic wands and other magical artifacts.
